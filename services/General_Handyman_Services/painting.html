The Perfect Finish: Pro Painting Tips

● The Secret is in the "Screeding"

A paint job is only as good as the wall underneath. For a premium Nigerian finish, we apply a thin layer of screeding putty and sand it to a mirror-smooth surface before the first drop of paint hits the wall.

● Matching Aged Paint

Paint changes color slightly as it ages. For touch-ups, our pros don't just use the original bucket; we "feather" the edges and blend the new paint into the old to ensure the repair is invisible.

● Dealing with Rising Damp

Painting over a damp patch is a temporary fix. We identify if it's "rising damp," apply a waterproof cement-based primer, and then paint. This ensures the finish won't peel again in 3 months.

● Cutting-In vs. Masking Tape

While amateur painters rely on tape, our pros use "cutting-in" techniques with angled brushes for razor-sharp lines where the wall meets the ceiling or skirting boards.
